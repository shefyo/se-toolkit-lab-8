## What is a backend

A backend is a server-side application that processes requests from [web clients](./web-infrastructure.md#web-client), implements business logic, and reads from or writes to a [database](./database.md#what-is-a-database). It exposes a [web API](./web-api.md#what-is-a-web-api) that clients communicate with over a [protocol](./computer-networks.md#protocol).

Docs:

- [Introduction to server-side programming](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Learn/Server-side/First_steps/Introduction)

## Service

A service is an application (or part of a system) that exposes [endpoints](./web-api.md#endpoint) and performs a focused responsibility.

A service can call other services over the [network](./computer-networks.md#what-is-a-network).

Examples:

- Authentication service.
- Recommendation service.
